Abstract#==#Despite the importance of the biomedical domain, there are few reliable
applications to support researchers and physicians for retrieving particular
facts that fit their needs. Users typically rely on search engines that only
support keyword- and filter-based searches. We present Olelo, a question
answering system for biomedicine. Olelo is built on top of an in-memory
database, integrates domain resources, such as document collections and
terminologies, and uses various natural
language processing components. Olelo is fast, intuitive and easy to use. We
evaluated the systems on two use cases: answering questions related to a
particular gene and on the BioASQ benchmark.
Olelo is available at: http://hpi.de/plattner/olelo.
